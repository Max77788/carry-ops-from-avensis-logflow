How It Works

From the carrier you trust
to the invoice you send.

Every step is connected. No re-keying. No phone tag. No scrambling at month-end. The platform is built around trust — carriers can't get on it without proving they belong.

1
Carriers join with built-in verification
Carriers go through digital onboarding — insurance, W-9, fleet list, drivers, safety record. The platform checks each item as it comes in. Expired insurance, missing docs, or failed basics? They don't get on. Period. By the time a carrier shows up in your bid invitations, they've already cleared the bar.
2
Drivers cleared and ready Optional
If you hire your own drivers, this step is yours. Run candidates through application, document collection, MVR, drug test, orientation, and training — all in one place, in English or Spanish. If your carriers bring their own drivers, skip it. Either way, the loads only roll with cleared drivers.
3
You post the load. The right carriers get invited.
Post your load — equipment, lane, tonnage, timeline. The platform automatically finds which verified carriers can actually run it and invites only them. No public board. No race to the bottom. Pick Open Bid mode, or pre-set the rate you're willing to pay.
4
Bids come in private. You award on the full picture.
Carriers submit their bid, or accept your set rate. They never see what the others bid. You see every offer alongside the carrier's safety score, on-time history, dispute count, and consistency rating. Award the load to the right combination — not just the cheapest number.
5
Pre-trip clears, then the truck rolls
Driver runs the pre-trip inspection on their phone. Photo capture, defect logging, voice notes — under two minutes. A critical fail locks dispatch automatically. Once cleared, the driver scans QR tickets at pickup, captures GPS and signatures at delivery. Everything timestamped, in English or Spanish.
6
Invoice generates itself. Records file themselves.
PDF tickets bundle into a billing package. Documents auto-archive for compliance. Optional bank tie-in moves cash faster. The cycle closes without anyone re-keying a single field.

The Differentiator

A bid board you control.
Not the carrier.

Public load boards put carriers in charge. Carry Ops flips it. You post the load. The platform invites only verified carriers who can actually run it. Bids are private — carriers can't see what each other bid. No race to the bottom. No shopping your rates around the market.

Two ways to award. Always your call.
Open Bid Mode
Set-Rate Mode

Open Bid — let qualified carriers bid privately. Set-Rate — pre-set the price you're willing to pay and send it to carriers who can fulfill. They accept or decline, no haggling. You stay in control either way.

Frequently Asked

Quick answers.

How is the Private Bid Board different from a public load board? +
Public boards expose your loads (and rates) to the entire market and put carriers in control. Carry Ops is invitation-only — you decide which verified carriers see the load, bids stay private from competing carriers, and you award on a combination of safety, capability, price, and consistency. No race to the bottom.
How long does it take to go live? +
Most operations are live within 60 days from kickoff. That includes carrier onboarding, e-ticketing, and the bid board for Growth-tier customers. Compare that to legacy TMS deployments that routinely take 6 to 12 months.
Will drivers actually use it? +
The driver workflow is mobile-first with big buttons, minimal typing, photo capture, voice notes, and full English/Spanish support. Tickets take under two minutes. Adoption sticks because it saves drivers time, not adds to it. Field resistance is the first thing the platform is designed against.
Can carriers refuse a set rate? +
Yes. In Set-Rate mode, you pre-set the price you're willing to pay and the platform sends the load to verified carriers who can fulfill. They accept or decline. No haggling. If no one accepts, you can switch to Open Bid mode or adjust the rate.
What about the bank integration? +
CashFlow Accelerator includes optional bank tie-in (PNC and others) that's enabled on shipper request. It accelerates collections by automating the POD-to-invoice-to-deposit flow. Available on the Enterprise tier and as an add-on.
Do you charge per load or per revenue? +
Pricing has three parts: a base platform fee, a flat $2 per load processed, and optional add-ons for the modules you actually use. The $2 per load is the same whether the load is worth $500 or $5,000 — it covers the platform infrastructure that processes the ticket, sends magic links to driver and destination, and archives the PDF for compliance. We don't take a percentage of your load value, and we don't charge based on your revenue.
What if I'm running 1,000+ loads a month? +
For high-volume operations, multi-yard shippers, brokers running custom integrations, or compliance-heavy industries (oilfield, hazmat) we build a custom Enterprise contract. That can include a volume-discounted per-load rate, white-glove onboarding, custom bank or ERP integrations, SLAs, and a dedicated success manager. Reach out and we'll build a quote tailored to your operation — there's no published rate card for Enterprise because no two operations are the same.
Who's behind Carry Ops? +
Carry Ops is a platform by AI FusionIQ Labs, an operational AI firm based in Houston with 25+ years of domain experience across transportation, oilfield, and field operations. The platform is built on real trucking reality — not adapted from a generic SaaS template.
